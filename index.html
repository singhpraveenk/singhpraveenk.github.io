Praveen Kumar Singh


I am a climate and atmospheric scientist dedicated to understanding the complex and rapidly evolving interactions between air quality, climate change, and cryospheric processes across the Hindu Kush Himalayan (HKH) region—one of the most climate‑sensitive and data‑scarce mountain systems on Earth. My research centers on the role of short‑lived climate pollutants, particularly black carbon, and how these particles influence regional climate dynamics, accelerate glacier melt, alter surface albedo, and modify radiative forcing patterns that ultimately affect downstream water resources and livelihoods.

To unravel these interconnected processes, I combine a diverse suite of modeling and observational approaches. My work spans general circulation models, regional climate models, radiative transfer simulations, radiation balance assessments, and hydrological modeling frameworks. These tools are complemented by ground‑based observations, satellite remote sensing, and high‑resolution gridded datasets, enabling a multi‑scale perspective that bridges atmospheric science, cryosphere research, and regional water‑balance studies. Through this integrated approach, I aim to generate actionable insights that support climate resilience, sustainable water management, and evidence‑based policy development across the HKH.
